## 1. Google Maps Is Where the Jobs Are


When someone searches:


- “emergency plumber Auckland”


- “electrician near me”


- “roof repair North Shore”


They are not browsing. They want someone to call now.


That traffic goes to Google Maps.


### What Determines Google Maps Rankings?


Your position in Google’s local 3-pack is influenced by:


**1. Google Business Profile optimisation**


- Correct categories


- Clear service descriptions


- Real photos of jobs


- Regular posts


- Accurate service areas


**2. Reviews**


- Quantity


- Recency


- Keywords inside reviews


- Owner replies


**3. Website authority**


- Linked properly to your profile


- Clear location signals


- Relevant service pages


If your Google Business Profile is half set up and your website says “we offer quality services”, you will not outrank competitors who structure it properly.

## 3. Why Most Trade Websites Don’t Rank


After reviewing dozens of Auckland trade websites, the issues are consistent.


### 1. No Dedicated Service Pages


One generic page that says:“We do plumbing, gas fitting, drainage, hot water cylinders…”


That won’t rank for “hot water cylinder replacement Auckland”.


Each service needs its own focused page.


### 2. Weak Page Structure


Most trade sites:


- Have one H1


- Minimal content


- No keyword alignment


- No structured headings


Google ranks clarity. Not pretty design.


### 3. No Real Content Depth


Google wants to understand:


- What you do


- Where you do it


- Who it’s for


- Why you’re different


Three paragraphs won’t cut it in competitive suburbs.


### 4. No Authority Signals


No case examples.No project images.No testimonials embedded into service pages.No FAQ content.


Google reads all of this.


## 4. The Structure That Actually Works for Trades


If you want consistent enquiries, your website needs to function like this:


### Step 1: Google Business Profile Optimised


- Primary trade category set correctly


- Service areas configured properly


- Weekly posts


- Review acquisition system


### Step 2: Proper Service Pages


Each page should include:


- Clear service keyword in title


- Auckland + suburb signals


- Real job photos


- FAQ section


- Strong call to action


- Internal links to related services


Example:


- Emergency Plumbing Auckland


- Hot Water Cylinder Replacement North Shore


- Switchboard Upgrade West Auckland


### Step 3: Internal Linking Strategy


Most trades sites ignore this.


Every service page should link to:


- Related services


- Main service hub


- Location pages


This builds authority across your site.


### Step 4: Conversion-Focused Layout


Traffic is useless if it doesn’t convert.


Your pages must include:


- Click-to-call buttons


- Clear above-the-fold offer


- Emergency messaging if relevant


- Social proof near CTAs


Ranking is half the battle. Conversion is the other half.

## Frequently Asked Questions About SEO for Trades



How long does SEO take for trades in Auckland?


Most trades businesses begin seeing measurable improvement within 3 to 6 months. Google Maps visibility can improve sooner if your profile and service pages are optimised correctly.





Is SEO better than Google Ads for electricians and plumbers?


Google Ads delivers immediate traffic, while SEO builds long-term visibility. The strongest strategy combines both to generate consistent enquiries.





Why is my trades website not ranking on Google Maps?


Most trade websites lack suburb-specific pages, keyword optimisation, and proper Google Business Profile integration. Without local signals, Google will not rank you consistently.





Do I need a new website to start SEO?


Not always. If your current site has technical or structural issues, updates may be required. In some cases, rebuilding with a lead-focused structure is more effective.





How much does SEO for trades cost in Auckland?


Costs vary depending on competition and service area. Trades businesses typically invest monthly to build rankings, improve Maps visibility, and generate consistent job enquiries.
