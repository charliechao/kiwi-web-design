### ❌ 1. No Google Search Visibility


Most people searching for a tradie, café, or local service in Auckland use Google, not Facebook. If you don’t have a website, your business won’t appear in search results when potential customers look for what you offer. That means you could be invisible to hundreds of people searching every month.


### ❌ 2. You Don’t Control Facebook


Facebook owns your page, not you. If the platform changes its rules, limits your reach, or even suspends your account, your business presence could disappear overnight. A website, on the other hand, is something you fully control.


### ❌ 3. Limited Professionalism


When customers compare two businesses-one with a professional website and one with only a Facebook page-they almost always trust the one with the website. A site gives your business credibility, shows you take your work seriously, and provides a clear way for customers to contact you.


### ❌ 4. Harder to Scale and Grow


Facebook pages are limited in functionality. You can’t easily integrate online bookings, payment systems, or advanced marketing tools. A website gives you flexibility to grow your digital presence over time-whether that means [SEO](/affordable-seo-auckland/), [e-commerce](/ecommerce-website-auckland/), or [Google Ads](/google-ads/).
