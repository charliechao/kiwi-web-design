# Are You Still Paying 2020 Prices for a Website in 2026?


![Website pricing in 2026 compared to traditional 2020 website development costs](https://www.kiwiwebdesign.co.nz/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/website-pricing-2026-ai-web-design-1024x624.png)


**AI Summary**


In 2026, AI-powered tools like vibe coding, Claude Code, Codex and Antigravity have dramatically reduced the time and cost of building websites. Many businesses are still paying 2020-style pricing based on outdated workflows. For most small business and informational websites, modern AI-assisted web design delivers faster results, lower costs, and the same or better quality.


If you built a website in 2020, the price probably made sense at the time.


Long timelines. Manual builds. Expensive developer hours. Every small change billed separately.


But it’s 2026 now, and the way websites are built has changed completely.


If you are still paying the same prices you were quoted years ago, you are likely overpaying.


### The Shift Most Businesses Missed


AI didn’t just speed things up. It rewired the entire web build process.


Modern tools like Claude Code, Codex, and Antigravity have made it possible to design and build high-quality websites and even web apps in a fraction of the time it used to take.


This is not about templates or low-quality shortcuts. It’s about removing waste.


What used to take weeks can now take days.What required large teams can now be handled by lean, AI-assisted workflows.What cost thousands in labour can now be delivered faster with better consistency.


Yet many agencies still price as if nothing has changed.


### Vibe Coding Changed the Game


Vibe coding is not hype. It is a real shift in how modern websites are produced.


Instead of writing everything line by line from scratch, AI assists with structure, layout, logic, and iteration. Designers and developers focus on direction, quality, and outcomes rather than mechanical work.


That means:


- Faster turnaround


- Fewer errors


- Lower build costs


- Easier updates and scalability


If your website is primarily informational or lead-driven, there is no logical reason it should cost what it did in 2020.


### What Should Cost Less Now (And Why)


Not all websites are the same, but many small business sites fall into this category:


- Service websites


- Local business sites


- Portfolio or brochure sites


- Landing pages


- MVP or validation websites


For these, AI-powered workflows make traditional pricing models outdated.


Paying old prices today usually means you are paying for inefficiency, not value.


### Where Pricing Still Makes Sense to Be Higher


Let’s be clear. Not everything should be cheap.


If your project involves:


- Complex strategy and positioning


- Custom systems and integrations


- E-commerce with advanced logic


- Ongoing experimentation and optimisation


Then pricing should reflect that responsibility.


The mistake businesses make is assuming every website falls into that category. Most do not.
